An Unwanted Passenger: Samsung’s Forced Bloatware Problem

Digital enthusiast, privacy advocate, and someone who really dislikes bloatware. Originally from Australia, now settled in Hamburg, but today’s topic hits close to home for users in West Asia and North Africa (WANA).

I’m passionate about lean tech—minimalist, efficient, and user-first solutions. So when I read about Samsung’s forced installation of the Israeli-founded AppCloud (via ironSource) on A and M series phones in the WANA region, it felt like a punch to the gut.

This isn’t just about bloatware—it’s about consent, transparency, and a company’s responsibility to its users. Let’s break it down.

The Problem: AppCloud’s Silent Invasion

  • No Opt-Out: The app is pre-installed, deeply embedded, and can’t be removed without voiding warranties or risking security.
  • Shadowy Data Collection: It hoovers up biometrics, IPs, device fingerprints—with zero clear privacy policies.
  • Legal & Ethical Red Flags: ironSource (now owned by Unity) is an Israeli company, raising concerns in regions where Israeli tech is restricted (e.g., Lebanon).

Samsung’s silence? Deafening.

Why This Matters

  1. Privacy ≠ Optional
    GDPR (and similar laws) exist for a reason. Users deserve to know what’s on their devices and how their data’s used.

  2. The IronSource Backstory
    ironSource has a history of shady practices—like adware bundling—making their presence on Samsung devices even more alarming.

  3. A Regional Double Standard
    Why force this in WANA but not elsewhere?

SMEX’s Open Letter: The Key Demands

SMEX, a Lebanese digital rights NGO, dropped a must-read open letter to Samsung. Highlights:

  • 📢 Disclose AppCloud’s privacy policy (where is it?).
  • 🛠️ Provide a real removal option—no root required.
  • ❌ Stop pre-installing it in future devices.

Full letter here.

My Take: A Call to Action

As someone who geeks out over lean systems and ethical tech, this is infuriating. Samsung’s a global leader—they can (and should) do better.

What you can do:
- 📱 Check your device (A/M series in WANA? Look for AppCloud).
- ✉️ Pressure Samsung—social media, support tickets, share SMEX’s letter.
- 🔥 Spread awareness—most users don’t even know this exists.

Final Thought

Tech should empower—not exploit. Here’s hoping Samsung listens before this blows up further.
